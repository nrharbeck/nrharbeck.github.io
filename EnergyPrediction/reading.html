Further Reading

For more information on project development, please check out the GitHub repository.

There are many good resources that provide additional details about renewable energy and energy efficiency policy in the U.S. Here are a few more places to check out:

  • American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy (ACEEE) - ACEEE is an advocate for EERE policies and has many resources on its website.
  • Consortium of Energy Efficiency (CEE) - CEE is a group of utilities that encourages energy efficiency and promotes efficiency targets that can be adopted into utility-level voluntary incentive programs.
  • Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency (DSIRE) - Database for many state-level policies used in this project.
  • U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and its Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE).
  • The Energy Information Administration (EIA) - EIA is a federal agency tasked with collecting energy statistics.
  • ENERGY STAR - A voluntary federal program that certifies technologies meeting certain efficiency levels. These levels are sometimes used for incentive programs by a variety of sectors.
